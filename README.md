POWERING UP
1st Power up the screen unit(s): 
Each Screen unit shows its assigned channel number.  
If all screens display the same channel proceed as normal.



2nd Power on the Controller

[Controller and Screen-unit(s) are turned on]

system proceeds through the boot-up sequence, displaying:

1)	Channel information
2)	The TimeJudge logo 
3)	The current (or last used) competition parameters
4)	The TimeJudge logo
CONTROL unit:
1)	Channel information
2)	Boot-up  WAIT message . . .
3)	. . .

From this HOME-screen (indicated by the TIME_TAP now message): 
•	Green <BTN1> The currently selected parameters can immediately be run as displayed.

Parameters may also be changed using Yellow <BTN 2> to enter Menu Mode and following the on-screen prompts:	
•	Yellow  buttons - Up / Down 	Scroll through the menu options: 	
•	Green <BTN 1>   		Accept a change:  			 
•	Red     <BTN 4>		Exit the menu mode to HOME:  	

TIME-TAP offers extra options (Flint round, IFAF round, etc) that are not available through the menu alone and are available to purchase on-line from our web site www.tjudge.com.

COUNTDOWN TIMER 
When a count-down to the tournament is required, or to run a mid-event break timer, there is a built-in on-screen count-down clock available.
1.	HOME screen
o	Yellow <BTN 3>  to select COUNTDOWN (fig 4):

2.	Duration Menu
o	Yellow UP/DOWN function (min: 3min, max: 4Hrs)  
NOTE:	<BTN 2> increments, 	+10 minute steps,  
             		<BTN 3> decrements,   	  - 1 minute steps.

3.	Green <BTN 1>   	begin COUNTDOWN: 	

o	Countdown will appear on the display screens.
	When the count reaches 1 minute:
	the screen will momentarily blank.
	show the parameter setup.
	flash a “PREPARE…” advisory for the archers.
 
	From approximately 45s to end of timer period the screen will blank, showing a scrolling waiting indication, and this condition will last indefinitely until the operator continues by pressing:
	Green <BTN 1> at the operator’s discretion to commence the shooting.


o	If the countdown timer is used for a mid-session break, the competition will automatically pick up where it left off.  
For example:  in a 12 End competition the break timer can be run after the 6th End. 
After the interval runs out the competition clock picks up at the beginning of the 7th End. 
  
During the countdown you may:
•	Change competition parameters: 	
o	using a Time-Tap card, you may alter the setup without pausing the timer. 
This is useful if for example the competition is moving from Qualifiers to Finals after a break.

•	Cancel the timer at any point: 
o	Red <BTN 4> will bring you back to the HOME screen and clear the timer	 
EMERGENCY STOP:  
Red <BTN 4> will “break” operations:

o	Green  <BTN 1>		RESUME play from elapsed time -10sec 
o	Yellow <BTN 2>		RESTART play from beginning of current END
o	Yellow <BTN 3>		RESET play to beginning of the current COMPETITION
o	Red Re<BTN 4>		OOPS: (Oh, damn! – I really did not mean that – please continue).


 

CHANGING CHANNELS:  LOCAL & GLOBAL options:
Channel selection is important where more than one set of timing screens are being used in proximity, or in the extremely rare instance where other (433MHz) RF devices may be causing interference (wireless intercoms, walkie talkies, talk-back units etc).

•	LOCAL channel change: To Change the channel on the CONTROLLER. 

NOTE:   This operation ONLY affects the CONTROLLER and does NOT affect any screen unit channels.

If the channel displayed on the Controller is not the chosen channel you may, immediately after powering up the Controller (fig 5):

o	Yellow <BTN 3>: 	 Enter Local Channel menu.
o	Yellow  UP/DOWN:   Select LOCAL channel
o	Green  <BTN 1>        ACCEPT change and PROCEED




•	GLOBAL channel change: 
If any screen(s) display a different channel when they are powered on, they will need to be changed to match the channel in use.


NOTE: 	This operation cannot be enabled without the Supervisor card; in this way the screen 		units' channel assignment cannot easily be altered in error.
		
Global Change example:
Three screens are to be used: Channel 6 is chosen as the working channel. (fig 6)
Here the screen-unit currently set to Ch 011 will first need to be changed to match the agreed frequency for the group: Ch 006   
Note:	There are 20 channels available from 001 in steps of 5: -   001, 006, 011 . . . 091, 096  

This is simple to do and is clearly explained below. 
1.	Pair the Controller to the unwanted channel (CH 011) :  see Local Channel Change (above).
2.	Green  <BTN 1>:         	ACCEPT change and PROCEED to HOME screen
3.	SUPERVISOR CARD:  	Enter GLOBAL CHANNEL CHANGE menu (fig 7)
4.	Yellow  UP/DOWN:		Select the desired channel (CH 006) 
5.	Green  <BTN 1>:		ACCEPT change & return to HOME 

This process will bring the channel on the Screen-unit and the Controller channel to the selected Ch 006, and can be repeated 
as needed to set any un-paired screens to the desired channel.
The control unit always powers up displaying the currently set Control channel number.
